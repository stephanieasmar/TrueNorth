COMPLIANCE




IMPLEMENTATION PROGRAM

True North Quality Consultants assist organizations in the development of an Implementation Program. The comprehensive Implementation Program is designed for companies in the early stages of the process. We make sure our clients are properly prepared for registration by tailoring our program to meet their particular needs.

The Implementation Program may include:

  • Gap Analysis, in which True North compares the organization’s current QMS, if any, to requirements, identifies nonconformities and recommends actions to achieve conformity.
  • Overview, describing QMS and registration requirements.
  • Quality Manual Development, creating a document that describes QMS policy, procedures, requirements, goals and structures.
  • Procedures Development, covering methods, strategies and principles used to perform QMS activities.
  • Registrar Liaison, contacting an accredited third-party firm that audits the QMS, its documentation and implementation for registration.
  • Management Representative Selection, assisting in choosing the person with day-to-day responsibility for QMS development and implementation, as well as liaison between management and the registrar.
  • Procedures Implementation, ensuring QMS conformity to the standard.
  • Internal Audit Assistance, to monitor QMS implementation for conformity to the standard.
  • Corrective Action Assistance, to eliminate QMS nonconformities found during internal audits.

MAINTENANCE PROGRAM

Once registration has been achieved, the quality management system (QMS) must be maintained. Our Maintenance Program can perform this work in a cost-effective and efficient manner, tailored to fit the client’s needs.

The True North Maintenance Program may include:

  • Internal Audits, which are a crucial component in QMS checks and balances, for discovering a major nonconformity during an internal audit can prevent registration from being revoked during the subsequent surveillance audit. True North can conduct internal audits twice a year in a professional, efficient and even-handed manner.
  • Corrective Actions, which are performed after surveillance audits to close out nonconformities.

The True North Maintenance Program frees up client personnel for regular job duties and costs less than committing a client employee to QMS upkeep.

DEFINITIONS

ISO 9000 is a set of international standards on quality management and quality assurance developed to help companies effectively document the quality system elements to be implemented to maintain an efficient quality system. They are not specific to any one industry and can be applied to organizations of any size. The ISO 9000 family of quality management systems standards is designed to help organizations ensure that they meet the needs of customers and other stakeholders while meeting statutory and regulatory requirements related to a product or service.

ISO 14001 is a standard related to environmental management that exists to help organizations minimize how their operations negatively affect the environment; comply with applicable laws, regulations, and other environmentally oriented requirements; and continually improve in the above. ISO 14001 is the international standard that specifies requirements for an effective environmental management system (EMS). It provides a framework that an organization can follow, rather than establishing environmental performance requirements.

ISO/TS 16949 is an ISO technical specification aimed at the development of a quality management system that provides for continual improvement, emphasizing defect prevention and the reduction of variation and waste in the automotive industry supply chain.

AS9100 is an aerospace standard based on the ISO 9001 quality system requirements. AS9100 takes the ISO9000 requirements and supplements them with additional quality system requirements, which are established by the aerospace industry in order to satisfy DOD, NASA and FAA quality requirements.

TL 9000 is a technical specification aimed at the development of a quality management system to meet the supply chain and operational quality requirements of the global information and communication technologies (ICT) industry. TL 9000 is built on ISO 9001 and its quality principles.

RoHS is the Restriction of Hazardous Substances Directive 2002/95/EC, (RoHS 1), short for Directive on the restriction of the use of certain hazardous substances in electrical and electronic equipment. The RoHS 1 directive took effect on 1 July 2006, and is required to be enforced and became a law in each member state. This directive restricts the use of six hazardous materials in the manufacture of various types of electronic and electrical equipment.
